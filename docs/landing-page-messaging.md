# Landing Page Messaging Template — StackLeaps for DMCs

**Purpose:** Defines every section of the landing page — structure, intent, and copy direction. Feed this to the AI builder alongside the visual design.

**Target audience:** DMC owners/managers worldwide. 74% want tour operator leads, 49% do MICE/events, 67% luxury-positioned, 51% explicitly B2B. They share: a destination to sell, a need for more inbound business, and no time for sales.

**Voice:** Personal, from Peretz. Direct, confident, casual — like the cold emails. Not agency-speak. Use their language: destinations, partners, meetings, operators, groups. Words that resonate with this audience: "vetted", "curated", "qualified."

**Core proof point:** 7 qualified meetings in 10 days. Present as established fact. Never say "first campaign." Never promise this as a guarantee on the page.

**Campaign alignment:** This page is where cold email recipients land. The emails lead with value ("I've vetted a short list of 10 operators active in your region"). The landing page should feel like the same person wrote it.

**A/B testing note:** This messaging is v1. Expect to change headlines, section order, and proof points based on real data. Nothing here is permanent.

## Section 2: Problem

**Purpose:** Name the pain. Make them feel seen. 3 cards maximum. Universal DMC pain — not niche-specific.

**Section label:** Sound familiar?
**Section headline:** Great Destinations Don't Sell Themselves

**Pain point 1: "Waiting for inbound that never comes"**
> You have an incredible destination — but the right clients don't know you exist. You're invisible to operators who'd love to work with you.

**Pain point 2: "Relying on word-of-mouth and hope"**
> Your best partnerships came from referrals or chance meetings. That's great — but it doesn't scale, and you can't control when the next one comes.

**Pain point 3: "You run operations AND chase new business"**
> You're managing ground logistics, client experiences, and supplier relationships — and somehow you're also supposed to be doing sales and marketing on top of it.

**Why these work:** They avoid trade-show-specific language. They resonate with a luxury DMC in Italy, an adventure DMC in Peru, and a MICE operator in Dubai equally. They mirror the email's empathetic tone.

## Section 3: Solution

**Purpose:** What we do, in 3 scannable points. Outcome-focused. Their language.

**Section headline:** We Do the Outreach. You Take the Meetings.

**Benefit 1: "We find the right partners in your target markets"**
> Vetted tour operators, travel advisors, and event planners — matched to your destination, your niche, and the type of groups you want.

**Benefit 2: "We reach out on your behalf — personalized, not spam"**
> Every message is tailored to the operator and your destination. No templates. No mass blasts. The kind of outreach that gets replies.

**Benefit 3: "Qualified introductions land on your calendar"**
> We handle everything from first contact to booking. You show up to the call with a partner who's already interested in what you offer. These aren't scraped lead lists — they're real conversations with decision-makers.

**Why these work:** Maps directly to the 3-step email sequence (find → reach out → book). Uses "vetted" for the luxury segment. "Qualified introductions" differentiates from lead lists (per offer research). Mentions "tour operators, travel advisors, and event planners" to cover 83%+ of the audience.

## Section 4: Process / How It Works

**Purpose:** Build credibility by showing a real system. Timeline format.

**Section headline:** From Onboarding to Meetings in Weeks

**Step 1: "We learn your destination"**
> 30-minute onboarding call. We understand your destination, your ideal partners, your specialization, and the source markets you want to grow in.

**Step 2: "We build your campaign"**
> We research and curate a list of operators in your target markets, write personalized outreach, and set up the infrastructure.

**Step 3: "Outreach goes live"**
> Your destination gets in front of the right people. Qualified responses come in. Meetings get booked on your calendar.

**Step 4: "We optimize what's working"**
> We track results, refine targeting, and double down on what converts. Your pipeline grows over time.

**Second CTA:** Same as hero CTA

## Section 5: Who This Is For

**Purpose:** Qualify by situation, not deal size. Makes the right prospects feel "this is built for me" — research shows qualifier sections increase conversion. Replaces the old "ideal fit" section that used deal-size thresholds.

**Section headline:** Is This a Fit?

**Qualifier 1:** You're a DMC that wants to grow your partner network but doesn't have time for outbound
**Qualifier 2:** You want qualified introductions to tour operators, travel advisors, or event planners in specific source markets
**Qualifier 3:** You'd rather focus on running your destination than chasing new business
**Qualifier 4:** You want a done-for-you service — not another tool or platform to manage

**Why situation-based:** A 5-person DMC in Montenegro and a 50-person MICE operator in Dubai both see themselves here. No deal-size or revenue filters that accidentally exclude large clients.

## Section 6: Founder / Trust

**Purpose:** Humanize. Build trust without testimonials. The video is the centerpiece — research says it should focus on the **unique mechanism** (how your method works), not your bio.

**Layout:**
- Video thumbnail (Peretz on camera, < 60 sec)
- Short text next to or below video

**Text draft:**
> I'm Peretz, founder of StackLeaps. I saw how hard it is for DMCs to connect with the right partners — trade shows are expensive, social media gets lost in the noise, and referrals don't scale. So I built a system that puts your destination directly in front of decision-makers at tour operators and event companies. You provide the destination expertise. We provide the introductions.

**What the video should cover (talking points, not script):**
1. Quick intro — who you are (10 sec)
2. The problem: how DMCs currently find partners and why it's broken (15 sec)
3. Your method: what you do differently — direct, personalized outreach to vetted operators (15 sec)
4. The offer: pay only for qualified introductions that happen, no retainer (10 sec)
5. What to do next: book the call (5 sec)

**Constraint:** Keep it real. No suit-and-tie corporate video. Talk like you'd talk to a DMC owner over coffee. The mechanism (how it works) builds more trust than credentials.

## Section 7: FAQ / Objection Handling

**Purpose:** Kill the remaining doubts. These are real objections from research + email reply patterns.

**Q1: "How is this different from what marketing agencies offer?"**
> Most agencies run ads or social media. We do direct, personalized outreach to specific operators and planners in your target markets. Every conversation is with someone who's already a potential fit for your destination.

**Q2: "Does cold outreach actually work in the travel industry?"**
> Yes — when it's relevant and personalized. We don't send mass emails. We reach the right person at the right company with a message about their business, not a generic pitch.

**Q3: "What if my destination is too niche?"**
> That's actually an advantage. The more specific your destination or specialization, the easier it is to find operators who are looking for exactly that. Your niche is the offer.

**Q4: "How much time do I need to invest?"**
> 30 minutes for the onboarding call. After that, we handle everything. You just show up to the meetings we book.

**Q5: "What if it doesn't work?"**
> You only pay for qualified introductions that actually happen. No retainer, no setup fee, no long-term contract. If we don't deliver, you don't pay.

**Q6: "What does it cost?"**
> Our pilot is pay-per-introduction — you only pay for qualified meetings that show up on your calendar. No retainer, no minimum commitment. We'll cover exact pricing on the strategy call based on your target markets and volume.
