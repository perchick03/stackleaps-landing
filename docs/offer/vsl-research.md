## 1. Does a 60-Second VSL Make Sense?

**Verdict: Yes, but it's a founder intro video, not a traditional VSL.**

### Evidence FOR 60 seconds
- **Funnel stage match:** The landing page receives cold outreach traffic (top-of-funnel). Research is clear: "Shorter videos should be used for awareness" and "explainer clips are great for building awareness in under 90 seconds." (Motionvillee)
- **One real-world test:** A company "replaced a 10-minute VSL with a 60-second explainer clip and increased engagement by 30%." (Motionvillee)
- **Attention window:** You have "3 to 5 seconds to capture attention before a prospect hits delete." (NLM Notebook 2, Hormozi/cold outreach data) -- 60 seconds respects busy DMC operators' time.
- **Video engagement data:** Pages with video hold visitors for 5 min 50 sec on average vs 43 seconds without video. (NLM Notebook 1) Even a short video dramatically extends time-on-page.
- **Conversion lift:** Video on landing pages increases conversions by up to 80-86%. (EyeView Digital, involve.me stats)

### Evidence AGAINST 60 seconds (and why it doesn't apply)
- Traditional VSL wisdom says "longer VSLs outperform short ones" because they handle objections thoroughly. (Jim Edwards, Copyblogger)
- Most VSLs are 5-10 minutes. (Skyline Social)
- **Why this doesn't apply:** Traditional VSLs replace the entire sales page. Our video sits alongside page copy that handles objections, proof, and details. The video's job is narrower: humanize, build trust, hook.

### Recommendation
**60 seconds is correct for this use case.** The video is a founder introduction on a cold-traffic landing page, not a standalone sales mechanism. The page copy carries the heavy selling. The video's job: make the prospect feel "this is a real person who understands my world."

## 2. Optimal Structure for 60 Seconds

Based on synthesis of the Jim Edwards VSL method, Hormozi value equation, and B2B-specific research:

### Beat-by-Beat Breakdown

| Time | Beat | What to say | Why |
|------|------|------------|-----|
| 0-5s | **Hook** | "I'm co-founder of a Balkans DMC — and I know how hard it is to find the right tour operator partners." | Insider credibility in the first 3 seconds. Not "I run an agency" — "I'm one of you." |
| 5-15s | **Problem (shared)** | "You're invisible to operators who'd love to work with you. Referrals don't scale. And you're already running operations — there's no time for outbound." | Name the universal pain. No trade show framing — this hits every DMC. |
| 15-25s | **Mechanism (what I built)** | "So I built an outreach system for our own DMC. We find tour operators, travel advisors, and planners who need destinations like yours — and we get you on a call with them." | "Built for our own DMC" = first-party proof. Explain just enough to be credible. |
| 25-35s | **Proof** | "7 qualified introductions in 10 days. 4 moved to sample itinerary stage. No trade show, no agency — just targeted, personalized outreach." | Specific numbers. "No trade show, no agency" differentiates without making trade shows the frame. |
| 35-50s | **Offer** | "We handle the research, the outreach, the follow-ups. You show up to conversations with operators who actually want to send travelers to your destination. And you only pay for meetings that happen." | Stack value + introduce pay-per-meeting. Minimize perceived effort. |
| 50-60s | **CTA** | "Scroll down to see how the Partner Pipeline Pilot works, or book a quick call and I'll show you what this looks like for your destination." | Named offer + dual CTA. |

**Total: ~150 words at natural speaking pace (2.5 words/sec)**

## 3. What Converts in B2B Founder Videos

### Talking Head vs. Produced
- **Talking head wins for cold traffic.** "People buy from people" (NLM Notebook 1). A founder on camera builds instant trust that polished production cannot replicate.
- **Professional quality still matters:** "Amateur videos don't add to your conversion goal" (NLM Notebook 1). Good lighting, clear audio, clean background. Not cinematic -- just competent.
- The sweet spot: **"professionally casual."** Well-lit, well-framed, but obviously a real person talking directly to camera. Not a voiceover, not a slide deck, not stock footage.

### Casual vs. Polished
- B2B buyers respond to **authority + approachability.** Too polished = "corporate video they'll skip." Too casual = "doesn't look like a real business."
- Recommendation: **Business casual appearance, direct eye contact, natural speech cadence.** Script should be memorized/internalized, not read from a teleprompter robotically.

### Problem-Led vs. Offer-Led
- NLM Notebook 2 (Hormozi framework) is clear: **Lead with the Dream Outcome, not just the problem.**
- But the hook must reference the problem to establish relevance.
- Structure: **Problem hook --> mechanism glimpse --> outcome proof --> offer.**
- "The 2-to-1 Rule: focus on the prospect's gain twice as much as your own accomplishments." (NLM Notebook 2)

### What NOT to Do
- Don't open with "Hi, I'm Peretz, and I'm the founder of StackLeaps." (NLM: you have 3-5 seconds, don't waste them on introductions)
- Don't use generic corporate language ("we leverage synergies")
- Don't show your product dashboard or technical setup (save for further down the page)
- Don't ask them to "sign up" or "get started" -- too high-friction for cold traffic

## 4. DMC-Specific Considerations

### What Resonates with Travel Industry Buyers
- **Visual proof matters enormously.** DMCs deal in experiences -- they respect visual storytelling. (NLM Notebook 3: "high-resolution images," "inspiring movies," "maps for geographic context")
- **They're used to being sold to at trade shows.** Your video should feel like a 1-on-1 conversation at a booth, not a keynote.
- **Seasonal pain is universal.** Every DMC owner knows the feast-or-famine cycle. Referencing it creates instant credibility.
- **They value insiders.** Research confirms DMCs prefer partners who "speak their language" — outsiders cause "huge delays." The co-founder angle gives you instant in-group trust.
- **They value relationships.** The travel trade runs on trust and personal connections. A founder video establishes that you're a person, not a faceless agency.
- **They're skeptical of tech/automation claims.** Don't lead with "AI-powered" or "automated system." Lead with the outcome (booked meetings with buyers), and let the mechanism be implied.

### Language That Works for DMCs
- "Tour operators" not "clients" or "customers"
- "Introductions" (see section 5 below)
- "Pipeline" -- they understand this from trade show context
- "Destinations like yours" -- positions their offering as unique
- "Qualified buyers" -- not "leads" (leads is our language, not theirs)

## 5. Key Questions Answered

### Should pricing be mentioned in the video?
**No.** Hormozi framework: "Your offer should be so unique that price comparisons are impossible." Mentioning price in a 60-second video before establishing full value is premature. Price belongs on the page below, after proof and value stacking, or on the call itself. The video's job is to get them to scroll or book -- not to qualify on budget.

### "Introductions" vs. "Meetings" -- which word for video?
**"Introductions" is better for the video.** Reasoning:
- "Meetings" implies a formal commitment and triggers resistance ("I don't have time for meetings")
- "Introductions" is softer, more relational, and aligns with how the travel trade works (warm intros at trade shows, fam trips, etc.)
- "Introductions" also sounds less like cold outreach/sales automation, which DMCs may be wary of
- **Exception:** When describing the Balkan Wanders proof point, "booked calls" is fine because it's a concrete metric. "7 booked calls" is more credible than "7 introductions."

### CTA: Book a call, scroll down, or something else?
**Dual CTA approach:**
1. **Primary (spoken in video):** "Scroll down to see how this works for your destination, or book a 15-minute call right now." -- gives two options, reduces friction
2. The video should NOT be the only path to conversion. It hooks, the page sells, the form/calendar converts.

Research supports this: "Multiple CTAs work when they evolve contextually." (Swipe Pages 2026) The video CTA is the first; page copy provides subsequent CTAs with increasing specificity.

**Avoid:** "Book a demo" (wrong framing -- you don't have a product to demo), "Sign up" (too transactional), "Learn more" (too vague).

### Talking head only or mixed with B-roll/screenshots?
**Talking head only for the 60-second version.** Reasoning:
- B-roll/screenshots add production complexity and delay launch
- The research says "show the product in action" matters for SaaS, but StackLeaps is a service -- there's no dashboard to show
- Talking head builds maximum trust for a service business
- **If you later want to upgrade:** A 10-second overlay of the Balkan Wanders campaign results (inbox screenshots, calendar bookings) at the proof beat (25-35s) would strengthen it. But this is a nice-to-have, not launch-critical.

## 6. Recommended Script Structure

### The "Founder Hook" Framework (60 seconds)

```
[HOOK - 0:00 to 0:05]
"I'm co-founder of a Balkans DMC — and I know how hard it is
to find the right tour operator partners."

[PROBLEM - 0:05 to 0:15]
"You're invisible to operators who'd love to work with you.
Referrals don't scale. And you're already running operations
— there's no time for outbound on top of everything else."

[MECHANISM - 0:15 to 0:25]
"So I built an outreach system for our own DMC. We find tour operators,
travel advisors, and planners who need destinations like yours
— and we get you on a call with them."

[PROOF - 0:25 to 0:35]
"7 qualified introductions in 10 days. 4 moved to sample itinerary
stage. No trade show, no agency — just targeted, personalized outreach
to the right people."

[OFFER - 0:35 to 0:50]
"We handle the research, the outreach, the follow-ups — everything.
You show up to conversations with operators who actually want to send
travelers to your destination. And you only pay for meetings that happen."

[CTA - 0:50 to 0:60]
"Scroll down to see how the Partner Pipeline Pilot works,
or book a quick call and I'll show you what this looks like
for your destination."
```

### Script Notes
- **Word count:** ~155 words. At a natural 2.5 words/second pace, this lands at ~62 seconds. Trim if needed by cutting adjectives, not sections.
- **Tone:** Confident but conversational. Like explaining to a DMC owner at a coffee meeting, not pitching from a stage.
- **Delivery:** Memorize the beats, not the exact words. Natural delivery > perfect recitation.
- **Eye contact:** Look directly at camera. Do not look at notes, a teleprompter scroll, or off to the side.
- **Setting:** Simple, clean background. A bookshelf or office is fine. Not a green screen, not outdoors, not a coworking space lobby.

### Technical Requirements
- **Resolution:** 1080p minimum
- **Audio:** External mic (lapel or desk mic). Built-in laptop/phone mic is not acceptable for B2B credibility.
- **Lighting:** Ring light or window light. Face should be evenly lit with no harsh shadows.
- **Framing:** Head and shoulders, slightly off-center. Leave room for a lower-third name overlay if desired.
- **Thumbnail:** First frame should show you mid-sentence with an engaged expression, not a static pose. Consider a custom thumbnail with text overlay: "I built this for my own DMC — here's how it works."

## 7. Implementation Priority

1. **Write and rehearse script** (30 min)
2. **Film 3-5 takes** (20 min)
3. **Pick best take, trim to 60s** (15 min)
4. **Add simple lower-third** with name/title (5 min in any editor)
5. **Embed on landing page** in hero section, muted autoplay with play button overlay OR click-to-play with compelling thumbnail
6. **Reiterate video's core points in page copy below** -- never assume everyone watches (NLM Notebook 1: "always reiterate core points from video script on the page in text")

**Total production time estimate: ~1.5 hours.** This is a launch-day asset, not a polished commercial. Ship it, measure, iterate.

---

## 8. Key Takeaways

1. **60 seconds is right** for cold-traffic founder intro. Not a full VSL -- a trust-building hook.
2. **Insider hook into outcome-proof** is the winning structure. "I'm co-founder of a DMC" beats any credential.
3. **Talking head, professionally casual.** No B-roll needed for launch.
4. **Don't mention pricing** in the video. Let the page or call handle it.
5. **Use "introductions" in the video,** "booked calls" for the proof metric.
6. **Dual CTA:** scroll down OR book a call.
7. **Ship fast.** Good enough on day 1 beats perfect on day 10. Video can be re-recorded anytime.
