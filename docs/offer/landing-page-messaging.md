# Landing Page Messaging — StackLeaps for DMCs

**Purpose:** Defines every section of the landing page — structure, intent, and copy direction. Feed this to the AI builder alongside the visual design.

**Offer alignment:** This page sells the **Partner Pipeline Pilot** (see dmc-offer-v2.md for full offer breakdown). $750 setup + $150/booked meeting. 3-call pilot to prove quality.

**Target audience:** DMC owners/managers worldwide. Primarily tour operator-facing, many also do MICE/events. They share: a destination to sell, a need for more inbound partner business, and no time for outbound sales.

**Voice:** Personal, from Peretz. Direct, confident, casual — like the cold emails. Not agency-speak. Use their language: destinations, partners, meetings, operators, groups. Words that resonate: "vetted", "qualified", "introductions."

**Core positioning:** Modern digital representation — not a marketing agency, not a lead list vendor. We're the rep company they wish they had in every source market, but with tracked results and pay-per-meeting accountability.

**Core proof point:** 7 qualified meetings in 10 days, built for our own DMC. First-party proof, not a client testimonial.

**Campaign alignment:** This page is where cold email recipients land. The emails lead with value ("I've vetted a short list of 10 operators active in your region"). The landing page should feel like the same person wrote it.

## Section 2: Problem

**Purpose:** Name the pain. Make them feel seen. 3 cards. Universal DMC pain — applies whether they attend trade shows or not.

**Section label:** Sound familiar?

**Section headline:** Great Destinations Don't Sell Themselves

**Pain point 1: "You're invisible to the operators who'd love to work with you"**
> You have an incredible destination — but the right tour operators don't know you exist. They're out there building programs in your region, looking for a ground partner. They just haven't found you.

**Pain point 2: "Referrals don't scale"**
> Your best partnerships came from chance meetings or word-of-mouth. That's great — but you can't control when the next one comes, and you can't grow a business on hope.

**Pain point 3: "You run operations AND chase new business"**
> You're managing ground logistics, client experiences, and supplier relationships — and somehow you're also supposed to be doing sales and marketing on top of it. There aren't enough hours.

**Why these work:** Universal to every DMC — a 5-person adventure operator in Peru and a 50-person MICE company in Dubai both feel this. No trade show framing. Mirrors the insider empathy of someone who lives the same problem.

## Section 3: Solution

**Purpose:** What we do, in 3 scannable points. Outcome-focused. Their language.

**Section headline:** We Do the Outreach. You Take the Meetings.

**Benefit 1: "We find the right partners in your target markets"**
> We map and vet tour operators, travel advisors, and event planners matched to your destination, your niche, and the type of groups you want. You get a custom ICP report of your 50 best-fit operators — yours to keep.

**Benefit 2: "We reach out on your behalf — personalized, not spam"**
> Every message is tailored to the operator and your destination. 14.5% positive reply rate on our own campaigns. That's because we understand the business — we run a DMC ourselves.

**Benefit 3: "Qualified introductions land on your calendar"**
> We handle everything from first contact to booking. You show up to the call with an operator who's already interested in what you offer. These aren't scraped lead lists — they're real conversations with decision-makers.

**Why these work:** Maps to the 3-step process. Introduces the ICP report early (tangible deliverable). The "we run a DMC ourselves" line drops the insider credibility naturally. "14.5% positive reply rate" is specific proof, not a vague claim.

**Purpose:** This is the strongest trust section. The insider angle IS the differentiator. Replaces a generic "about us."

**Section headline:** Built for Our Own DMC First

**Text:**
> I'm Peretz, co-founder of a Balkans DMC. My partner runs ground operations — I built the outreach system that fills our calendar with tour operator meetings.
>
> 7 qualified introductions in 10 days. 4 moved to sample itinerary stage. 2 became serious partners. No trade show. No agency. Just targeted, personalized outreach to the right operators.
>
> When other DMC owners started asking how we did it, I realized the system wasn't destination-specific — it was methodology-specific. So I opened it up, one destination at a time.

**Video:** Founder video (< 60 sec) — talking points:
1. Quick intro — who you are, the Balkans DMC connection (10 sec)
2. The problem you lived — invisible to operators, no time for outbound (15 sec)
3. What you built — personalized outreach system, how it works (15 sec)
4. The results — 7 meetings, 4 itineraries, 2 partners (10 sec)
5. The offer — pilot with 3 calls to prove quality, pay per meeting (5 sec)
6. CTA — book the call (5 sec)

**Constraint:** Talk like you'd talk to a DMC owner over coffee. No suit-and-tie corporate video. The mechanism (how it works) + the insider story (I live this too) builds more trust than credentials ever could.

## Section 8: FAQ / Objection Handling

**Purpose:** Kill the remaining doubts. These are real objections from pilot email replies + research.

**Q1: "How is this different from a marketing agency?"**
> Most agencies run ads or social media. We do direct, personalized outreach to specific operators and planners in your target markets. And we're not outsiders — we run a DMC ourselves, so we understand what a qualified partner actually looks like.

**Q2: "Does cold outreach actually work in travel?"**
> It does when it's relevant and personalized. We don't send mass emails. We had a 14.5% positive reply rate on our own DMC's campaign — because each message is about the operator's business, not a generic pitch.

**Q3: "What if my destination is too niche?"**
> That's actually your advantage. The more specific your destination or specialization, the easier it is to find operators who are looking for exactly that. Your niche is the offer.

**Q4: "How much time do I need to invest?"**
> 30 minutes for the onboarding call. After that, we handle everything. You just show up to the meetings we book.

**Q5: "What if it doesn't work?"**
> You pay $750 for the ICP research and campaign setup — that's yours to keep regardless. We then deliver 3 qualified introductions. If you don't like the quality, you walk away. No contract, no obligation.

**Q6: "What does it cost?"**
> $750 setup for your custom ICP analysis and campaign infrastructure, then $150 per qualified meeting that shows up on your calendar. No retainer, no minimum commitment.

**Q7: "Don't you compete with me? You run a DMC."**
> We're in the Balkans — and we only work with one DMC per destination. Your campaign data, targeting, and strategy are completely confidential. We only get paid when you get meetings — our interests are aligned.

**Q8: "I've been burned by agencies before."**
> That's exactly why there's no contract and no retainer. $750 for the research, 3 meetings to prove quality. If it's not there, clean exit. We take the risk, not you.

## Research Validation Notes

- **Landing Page notebook (49 sources):** PAS structure validated, proof-near-CTA validated, Calendly embed validated, process timeline validated. Applied: shortened H1 options, added qualifier section, proof line near CTA.
- **Offer notebook (27 sources, Hormozi):** Grand Slam framework applied. Hybrid pricing validated. Trial/pilot confirmed as strongest risk reversal for B2B. "Charging for trial gets more serious buyers than free."
- **DMC Cold Outreach notebook (29 sources):** Universal pain = disintermediation + invisibility. Insider positioning validated — DMCs prefer partners who "speak their language." Confirmed: outsiders cause "huge delays."
- **DMC ICP notebook (28 sources):** Representation companies validated as positioning anchor. Insider trust is "fundamental currency" of DMC industry. Destination exclusivity eliminates conflict-of-interest concern.
- **Pilot UK v2 data:** Boutique/small group (+30% lift), activity-niche (+19%), wine/food (+21%), multi-destination (+19%). "Luxury" alone is slightly negative. Tour operators convert at 48% vs pure boutique advisors at 29%.
